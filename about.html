Introducing Dorothea Perkins, a vibrant 30-year-old native of Atlanta, my hometown. My journey into the world of technology ignited with an early fascination for computers, evolving into a deep-seated passion for helping others. Rooted in my love for the community, I dedicate my time to volunteering at The Campbellton Community Garden and The Cobb County Dog Shelter, fostering connections and making a positive impact.


I currently serve as the president of the Women in Cybersecurity Club at Kennesaw State University, actively championing inclusivity and empowerment within the tech realm. Whether troubleshooting a system glitch or collaborating on innovative solutions, the thrill of problem-solving drives my commitment to excellence. My short-term goal involves graduating with a Bachelor's degree in Information Technology, specializing in cybersecurity.


My journey is a testament to the transformative power of education and community engagement. As I navigate the intricate world of cybersecurity, I am not only focused on personal success but driven by a broader mission to create opportunities for others. Through my experiences, I've come to believe that empowering individuals, particularly young Black girls, with the tools and mentorship needed to thrive in the cybersecurity field can lead to lasting change.
